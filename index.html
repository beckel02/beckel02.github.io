Common questions

What is an AZA accredited zoo?

Basically what being an AZA accredited zoo mean is that the zoo has agreed to conduct themselves a certain way. They also have agreed to help educate the public, and help conservation efforts, among other things. An AZA accredited zoo also has access to information from other AZA accredited zoos. Information can range from why did you choose to set up your lion enclosure that way, to what is your polar bears favorite treat. This network of peers can prove to be extremely helpful. To learn more about AZA accreditation visit the AZA webpage.

Who are zookeepers?

Zookeepers are professionals who have at least a bachelors in an animal related field. Their job is to take care of the animals. This ranges from cleaning the enclosures to making their diets. One of the more fun aspects of the job is enrichment. This is something that changes up the animals day and helps keep them active. Keepers are generally in charge of choosing enrichment for the animal which can range from a new scent to a new toy with treats hidden inside.
